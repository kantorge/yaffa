# Investment Transactions

## Feature Summary

Investment transactions represent actions that change an investment position or record income generated by an investment. They extend the transaction concept into long-term asset tracking while still remaining part of the same financial timeline.

This part of the model is more specialized than standard cashflow entry and is aimed at users who want to track holdings, purchases, sales, and yield.

## Target User

- Primary:
  Advanced or detail-oriented users tracking investment activity alongside normal finances.

- Secondary:
  Users performing periodic portfolio review or correcting holding history.

## User Problem

- Investment activity cannot be described accurately with the same fields as normal income or spending.
- Users need to track both cash impact and asset quantity impact.
- Some investment events, such as dividends or manual share corrections, are not traditional trades but still matter financially.

## User Value / Benefit

- Brings investment tracking into the same system as the rest of the user’s finances.
- Helps users see both portfolio movement and related cashflow.
- Supports more realistic long-term planning and performance review.

### Functional Benefits

- Supports trade events, position corrections, and income events.
- Separates quantity changes from cashflow changes when needed.
- Supports recurring investment transactions through scheduling.

### Conceptual Benefits

- Helps users understand investing as part of their overall financial behavior, not as a separate silo.
- Preserves the mental distinction between owning more shares and receiving cash from an investment.

## Technical Description

Investment transactions use a dedicated investment detail model containing:

- account
- investment
- price
- quantity
- commission
- tax
- dividend or yield amount

Every investment transaction is attached to both an account context and a specific investment asset, and they must share the same currency.

Additionally, the user can set:

- date or schedule configuration
- optional comment

## Subtype Breakdown

### Buy

Represents purchasing an investment.

User meaning:

- cash is spent
- holdings increase

Required properties:

- account
- investment
- price
- quantity

Optional cost adjustments:

- commission
- tax

### Sell

Represents selling an investment.

User meaning:

- cash is received
- holdings decrease

Required properties:

- account
- investment
- price
- quantity

Optional cost adjustments:

- commission
- tax

### Add Shares

Represents a manual increase in holdings without a normal purchase event.

User meaning:

- holdings go up
- this may reflect a correction, bonus issue, or other non-trade adjustment

Required properties:

- account
- investment
- quantity

Optional properties:

- commission
- tax

### Remove Shares

Represents a manual decrease in holdings without a normal sale event.

User meaning:

- holdings go down
- this may reflect a correction or other non-trade adjustment

Required properties:

- account
- investment
- quantity

Optional properties:

- commission
- tax

### Dividend

Represents cash income paid by an investment.

User meaning:

- holdings stay the same
- cash income increases

Required properties:

- account
- investment
- income amount

Optional properties:

- commission
- tax

### Interest Yield

Represents yield or interest-like income associated with an investment or interest-bearing asset.

User meaning:

- holdings stay the same
- income increases

Required properties:

- account
- investment
- income amount

Optional properties:

- commission
- tax

## Cashflow vs Quantity Logic

| Type           | Cashflow                         | Quantity |
| -------------- | -------------------------------- | -------- |
| Buy            | negative                         | positive |
| Sell           | positive                         | negative |
| Add shares     | neutral or not directly monetary | positive |
| Remove shares  | neutral or not directly monetary | negative |
| Dividend       | positive                         | none     |
| Interest yield | positive                         | none     |

This distinction is important because YAFFA uses investment transactions to describe both account-level money movement and position-level holding changes.

## Inputs

- transaction subtype
- investment account
- target investment
- quantity and/or price depending on subtype
- optional commission and tax
- date or optional schedule settings
- optional comment

## Outputs

- stored investment history
- changes in quantity timeline
- cashflow effects where applicable
- data for portfolio and account-level summaries

## Core Logic / Rules

- Every investment transaction belongs to an account and an investment.
- Buy and sell require both price and quantity.
- Add shares and remove shares require quantity but not a normal trade price.
- Dividend and interest yield require a positive income amount.
- Investment transactions support scheduling, but budgeting is not the primary mode exposed for them.
- Reconciled status is not compatible with scheduled investment entries.

## User Flow

1. User chooses an investment transaction type.
2. YAFFA shows the relevant fields for that subtype.
3. User enters quantity, price, or income information as needed.
4. The transaction is saved as a historical record or as a recurring scheduled template.
5. The transaction contributes to holding history and cashflow analysis.

## Edge Cases / Constraints

- Not all investment transactions imply both cash and quantity movement.
- Manual share adjustments should not be confused with trades.
- Income events are part of the investment timeline even when no holdings change occurs.

## Dependencies

- Models:
  - Transaction
  - TransactionDetailInvestment
- Related concepts:
  - Account
  - Investment

## Frontend Interaction

- Investment forms expose a subtype selector and then conditionally show price, quantity, and income fields.
- Scheduling is available for planned recurring investment activity.
- The form intentionally differs from the standard cashflow form because the user intent is different.

## Confidence Level

High

## Assumptions

- The same investment detail field is reused for dividend and interest-style income amounts in the implementation.
- Users care more about the meaning of the action than about the internal storage model.
